The Mission (If You Can Call It That)

We are the Ancient Order of Absurdity. We exist because everything else is ridiculous, so why not lean into it?

We started as a meme. A joke about jokes. A token that tokenizes the tokenization of nothing in particular. But here's the thing about absurdity—it has a way of becoming accidentally profound.

Our first job: to be the friend to all fair memes on SUI and beyond. To accelerate the weird, the wonderful, and the utterly pointless. Our second job: to empower the artists, the weirdos, the filmmakers—the ones with no voice—to say and do great, absurd things. We make no promises. It would be absurd to.

For now, just enjoy the ride. It's all ridiculous anyway.

The Un-Roadmap

Or: How to Plan for the Unplannable

Traditional roadmaps are for traditional projects. We are not traditional. Here's what might happen, probably will happen, or could happen if the stars align and someone remembers to do it:

1

Phase 1: The Launch

Deploy on SUI via SRM's Lotus Launch. Build the initial community. No influencers, no paid pumps—just pure, organic chaos. If you're here, you're early. Congratulations on your questionable life choices.

2

Phase 2: The Gathering

Community growth through memes, madness, and mutual appreciation of the absurd. Monthly meme contests with extra rewards. Encourage members to host Twitter Spaces. Register on various platforms because apparently that's what you do.

Phase 3: The Evolution

If the community wants it, we evolve. Polls for bigger initiatives. Grants for filmmakers, musicians, artists—the voices that usually get drowned out. But only if the treasury is fat and the community demands it. No promises.

∞

Phase ∞: The Unknown

We adapt. We grow. We change with the seasons and the memes. Members will come and go, but the culture of absurdity endures. This is a meme project—don't expect extra utility unless the community creates it.

Remember: this is community-driven. We provide the framework; you provide the chaos. The tokenomics make it sustainable, but you make it meaningful.
